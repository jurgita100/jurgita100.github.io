Leonard Cohen

I have tried in my way to be free

Here's a time line of Leonard Cohen life:

  • 1934 - Born i Westmount, Canada
  • 1948 - After receiving elementary education at the ‘Roslyn Elementary School’ he joined the ‘Westmount High School’. During high school he learnt poetry and music, and took special interest in the compositions of Spanish poet Federico Garcia Lorca.
  • 1955 - He completed his bachelor’s degree in arts.
  • 1956 - He published a book of his poems written during his teenage, named ‘Let Us Compare Mythologies’. This poet dedicated the book in memory of his late father.
  • 1957 - Leonard Cohen went back to Montreal, Canada, where he engaged in some jobs and simultaneously, continued his literary works penning poems and fictional stories.
  • 1961 - His collection of poetry entitled ‘The Spice-Box of Earth’ was published by the publishing house ‘McClelland & Stewart’ of Canada. The book was received well by the readers and helped Cohen enter the poetic world of Canada.
  • 1967 - His musical career got a kick-start when his album ‘Songs of Leonard Cohen’ was released. This album received acclamations and featured on the charts in the UK even after a year of its release. This was followed by his albums ‘Songs From a Room’ and ‘Songs of Love and Hate’.
  • 1975 - He toured the US and Canada along with Lissauer to endorse his new album ‘The Best of Leonard Cohen’. The following year, he went on a tour to Europe to promote the same album.
  • 1977 - His album ‘Death of a Ladies’ Man’ was produced and a year later, a book by the same name was printed.
  • 1979 - His album ‘Recent Songs’ was released and with this album he turned a producer.
  • 1979 - His album ‘Recent Songs’ was released and with this album he turned a producer.
  • 1984 - His collection of poems ‘Book of Mercy’ hit the book stores and this book was immensely successful, and the poet was highly appreciated. The same year, his album ‘Various Positions’ was released, which was followed by the release of his eighth studio album ‘I’m Your Man’.
  • 1991 - This talented artist was inducted into the ‘Canadian Music Hall of Fame’.
  • 1993 - He won the ‘Juno Award’ for Best Male Vocalist in 1993. The same year, he was awarded with the ‘Governor General's Performing Arts Award’ for Lifetime Artistic Achievement.
  • 1994 - The ‘Juno Award’ for Songwriter of the Year was won by him.
  • 2003 - He was felicitated by the ‘Order of Canada’.
  • 2007 - He won the ‘Grammy Award’ for Album of the Year.
  • 2008 - He was inducted in the ‘Rock and Roll Hall of Fame’ by Lou Reed. The same year, he was made the Grand Officer of the ‘National Order of Quebec’.
  • 2010 - He this artist was awarded with the ‘Grammy Lifetime Achievement Award’. The same year, he was inducted into the ‘Songwriters Hall of Fame’.
  • 2011 - Leonard received the ‘Prince of Asturias Award for literature’. The following year, Cohen was awarded with the Inaugural 'PEN Award for Song Lyrics of Literary Excellence' and the 'Prix Denise-Pelletier'.
  • 2012 - He also recorded an album ‘Old Ideas’.
  • 2013 - He was awarded the Artist of the Year at the ‘Juno Award’.
  • 2014 - He worked on another album ‘Popular Problems’ which went on to win the Album of the Year award at the ‘Juno Awards’ the following year.
  • 2015 - He began working on yet another album, ‘You Want It Darker.’
  • 2016 - Dies at age of 82.
